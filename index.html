Overview

Digital Twins (DTs) are world models of real-world environments that are used for planning, training, optimisation, and control of their real-world counterparts. More efficient digital twin models of environments can now be created by leveraging recent advancements in 3D Gaussian Splatting methods, foundation models, and generative AI techniques. An important component of DTs is the dynamic tracking of objects, which has been significantly enhanced by recent progress in object tracking and segmentation. Furthermore, constructing accurate models of articulated and flexible objects is essential, and the latest innovations offer significant improvements in this area. This workshop aims to attract researchers and industrial partners who are bridging the gap in programming intelligent agents to perform tasks in home environments.

  • How can we initialise a scene in realtime using neural representations?
  • What should a digital twin neural representation capture (geometry, visual appearance, dynamics, etc.) for robotics?
  • How can we use these digital twins for robot manipulation?
  • How to benchmark the progress in digital twin construction for robotics?
